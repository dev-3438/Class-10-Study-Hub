1. Why Do Organisms Reproduce?

Reproduction = making more organisms of the same type. But why tho? Simple: Every organism eventually dies (sad but true). So the species must continue. Reproduction ensures life doesn’t get Thanos-snapped out of existence. Think of it like: Your phone battery drains → you recharge to keep it going. Species also “recharge” themselves by reproduction.

2. Modes of Reproduction

Only one parent is needed.
The new organism looks exactly like the parent (clone).

Types of Asexual Reproduction:

1. Binary Fission
Parent splits into two equal parts.
Example: Amoeba, Bacteria.

2. Multiple Fission
Many new organisms are formed inside a protective covering.
Example: Plasmodium.

3. Budding
A small bud grows on the parent and becomes a new organism.
Examples: Hydra, Yeast.

4. Fragmentation
Body breaks into pieces, and each piece grows into a new organism.
Example: Spirogyra.

5. Regeneration
Some organisms can regrow lost parts, and even a whole body can grow from a part.
Example: Planaria.

6. Vegetative Propagation (Plants)
New plants grow from stems, roots, or leaves.
Examples: Potato (tuber), Onion (bulb), Money plant (cuttings).

7. Spore Formation
Spores are tiny, thick-walled cells that grow into new organisms in good conditions.
Examples: Fungi, Bread mould.

B. Sexual Reproduction

Two parents are involved.
Male and female gametes fuse to form a new individual.
This leads to variation.

3. Sexual Reproduction in Plants

Parts of a Flower:

Male part – Stamen
– Anther: makes pollen grains
– Filament: supports the anther

Female part – Carpel/Pistil
– Stigma: receives pollen
– Style: connects stigma to ovary
– Ovary: contains ovules

Pollination
Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma.
Types:
– Self-pollination
– Cross-pollination

Agents: wind, water, insects, birds

Fertilization
– Pollen lands on stigma
– Pollen tube grows down the style
– Male gamete reaches ovule
– Fusion forms zygote

After fertilization:
– Ovary becomes fruit
– Ovule becomes seed
– Zygote becomes embryo

4. Human Reproduction

Male Reproductive System
– Testes: produce sperm
– Scrotum: keeps testes cool
– Vas deferens: carries sperm
– Prostate gland & seminal vesicles: add fluid
– Urethra: passage for semen

Female Reproductive System
– Ovaries: produce eggs
– Fallopian tubes: place where fertilization happens
– Uterus: baby develops here
– Cervix: opening of uterus
– Vagina: birth canal

Menstrual Cycle (about 28 days)
– Uterus lining grows
– Egg is released around day 14
– If not fertilized, lining breaks → periods

Fertilization in Humans
– Sperm meets egg in fallopian tube
– Forms zygote → embryo
– Embryo attaches to uterus
– Baby grows with help of placenta

5. Reproductive Health

– Good hygiene
– Awareness
– Protection from STDs
– Family planning

Contraceptive methods:
– Condoms
– Birth control pills
– Copper-T
– Vasectomy/Tubectomy

6. Variation
Variation comes from sexual reproduction.
It helps species survive changes in the environment.
