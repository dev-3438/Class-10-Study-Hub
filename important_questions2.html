Question 1

3 Marks

Define fertilisation. Distinguish between external fertilisation and internal fertilisation. Also, state the site of fertilisation in human beings.

Answer:

Fertilisation is the fusion of a male gamete (sperm) with a female gamete (ovum or egg) to form a zygote.
External fertilisation: Fusion of gametes takes place outside the body of the parent; gametes released into water. Example: Frogs, fishes.
Internal fertilisation: Fusion of gametes occurs inside the female body. Example: Humans, birds.
In humans, fertilisation occurs in the fallopian tube.

Question 2

3 Marks

What is vegetative propagation? Give two advantages of vegetative propagation.

Answer:

Vegetative propagation is a method of asexual reproduction in plants where new plants grow from roots, stems, or leaves instead of seeds.
Advantages:
1. It is a quick and simple method of producing new plants.
2. Offspring are genetically identical to parent plants, preserving desirable traits.

Question 3

4 Marks

Explain the process of pollination and fertilisation in flowering plants. What happens to ovary and ovule after fertilisation?

Answer:

Pollination: Transfer of pollen grains from anther to stigma. Can be self-pollination or cross-pollination. Agents: wind, water, insects, birds.
Fertilisation: Pollen germinates on stigma, pollen tube grows through style into ovary, male gamete fuses with female gamete (ovule) forming zygote.
After fertilisation: Ovule → Seed, Ovary → Fruit, Zygote → Embryo.

Question 4

3 Marks

What is the role of testes and vas deferens in the human male reproductive system?

Answer:

Testes produce sperm and male sex hormones (testosterone).
Vas deferens transports sperm from testes to urethra.

Question 5

4 Marks

Explain the menstrual cycle. What happens if the egg is not fertilised?

Answer:

The menstrual cycle lasts about 28 days.
- Uterus lining builds up preparing for pregnancy.
- Egg is released around day 14 (ovulation).
If the egg is not fertilised, the lining breaks down and is shed through menstruation.

Question 6

3 Marks

What is the role of placenta during pregnancy?

Answer:

Placenta connects the developing embryo to the uterus.
It supplies oxygen and nutrients to the embryo and removes waste products.
Acts as a protective barrier and hormone producer for pregnancy.

Question 7

3 Marks

Why is variation important in sexually reproducing organisms?

Answer:

Variation ensures offspring are not identical, which helps survival in changing environments.
It increases adaptability and evolution of the species.

4 Marks

Differentiate between asexual and sexual reproduction.

Answer:

Feature Asexual Reproduction Sexual Reproduction
Number of Parents Single parent Two parents
Gamete Formation No gametes Male and female gametes formed
Genetic Variation Offspring identical to parent Offspring genetically varied
Examples Binary fission, budding, vegetative propagation Fertilisation in plants and animals
