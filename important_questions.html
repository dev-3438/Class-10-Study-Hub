Important Board Exam Questions

Light - Reflection and Refraction (3-4 Marks Each)

Question 1

3 Marks

State the laws of reflection of light. Explain with the help of a ray diagram, the image formed by a plane mirror.

Answer:

Laws of Reflection:
  1. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection (∠i = ∠r)
  2. The incident ray, the normal to the mirror at the point of incidence, and the reflected ray all lie in the same plane
Image Formation by Plane Mirror:
  • Image is formed behind the mirror at same distance as object
  • Image is virtual, erect, and same size as object
  • Image is laterally inverted (left appears right, right appears left)

Ray Diagram Description: Draw incident rays from object to mirror, show reflected rays extending behind mirror where they appear to meet forming virtual image.

Question 2

4 Marks

An object 4 cm high is placed 30 cm in front of a concave mirror of focal length 20 cm. Find the position, nature, and size of the image formed.

Answer:

Given:
Object height (h) = 4 cm
Object distance (u) = -30 cm (negative as per sign convention)
Focal length (f) = -20 cm (concave mirror)
Using Mirror Formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/(-20) = 1/v + 1/(-30)
-1/20 = 1/v - 1/30
1/v = -1/20 + 1/30
1/v = (-3 + 2)/60 = -1/60
v = -60 cm
Finding Magnification:
m = -v/u = -(-60)/(-30) = -2
m = h'/h = -2
h' = -2 × 4 = -8 cm
Results:
• Position: 60 cm in front of mirror (real image)
• Nature: Real, inverted (negative sign)
• Size: 8 cm (magnified, since |m| = 2 > 1)

Question 3

3 Marks

Define refractive index. Explain why a coin placed at the bottom of a water tank appears raised when viewed from above.

Answer:

Refractive Index Definition:
The refractive index of a medium is the ratio of speed of light in vacuum to the speed of light in that medium.
Formula: μ = c/v
Why Coin Appears Raised:
  • This phenomenon occurs due to refraction of light
  • Light rays from the coin travel from water (denser) to air (rarer)
  • When light enters rarer medium, it bends away from the normal
  • Our brain assumes light travels in straight line
  • So, the coin appears to be at a higher position than its actual position
  • This is called apparent depth - real depth > apparent depth

Real-life examples: Fish in aquarium appear closer, swimming pool appears shallower than it actually is.

Question 4

4 Marks

A convex lens has a focal length of 25 cm. Calculate the power of the lens. Where should an object be placed to get a real, inverted image of the same size as the object?

Answer:

Part 1: Calculating Power
Given: f = 25 cm = 0.25 m
Power P = 1/f = 1/0.25 = +4 D
The lens has power of +4 dioptres
Part 2: Finding Object Position
For real, inverted image of same size: m = -1
m = -v/u = -1, therefore v = u

Using lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
Since v = u: 1/f = 1/u - 1/u = 0
This gives infinite focal length, which is not possible.

Correction: For same size real image, object should be at 2F.
So, u = 2f = 2 × 25 = 50 cm
Then v = 2f = 50 cm (at 2F on other side)
Final Answer:
• Power = +4 D
• Object should be placed at 50 cm from the lens (at 2F)

Question 5

3 Marks

Draw ray diagrams to show the image formation by a convex lens when the object is placed (a) between F and 2F, and (b) at F. State the nature of image in each case.

Answer:

(a) Object between F and 2F:
  • Draw ray parallel to principal axis - passes through F after refraction
  • Draw ray through optical center - passes undeviated
  • Rays meet beyond 2F on other side
  • Nature of image: Real, inverted, and magnified
  • Position: Beyond 2F on other side
(b) Object at F:
  • Draw ray parallel to principal axis - passes through F after refraction
  • Draw ray through optical center - passes undeviated
  • Both rays become parallel after refraction
  • Nature of image: Real, inverted, and highly magnified
  • Position: Formed at infinity

Key Point: When object moves from infinity towards focus, image moves from focus to infinity and keeps getting larger!
