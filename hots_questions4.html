Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS)

Advanced Questions - Light Reflection and Refraction

Question 1

HOTS

Explain why diffusion is sufficient for unicellular organisms but multicellular organisms require a transport system. Give examples.

Answer: Unicellular organisms are in direct contact with the environment; gases and nutrients diffuse directly across the cell membrane. Multicellular organisms have many internal cells far from the environment, so diffusion is insufficient; transport systems (blood, xylem/phloem) are required.
Example: Amoeba vs Human circulatory system.

Question 2

HOTS

Why do herbivores have longer small intestines than carnivores? Relate it to their diet and digestion efficiency.

Answer: Herbivores consume cellulose-rich plant material, which is hard to digest. A longer small intestine provides more time and surface area for enzymatic digestion and absorption. Carnivores eat proteins and fats, which digest faster, so shorter intestines are sufficient.
Example: Cow vs Lion.

Question 3

HOTS

During heavy exercise, muscles sometimes cramp. Explain why anaerobic respiration occurs and its effect on muscle cells.

Answer: Low oxygen supply forces muscles to switch to anaerobic respiration, producing energy and lactic acid. Accumulated lactic acid causes fatigue, pain, and cramps.
Example: Sprinting or weightlifting leads to lactic acid buildup in leg muscles.

Question 4

HOTS

Explain how oxygen reaches all body cells and how nutrients from digested food are transported to cells.

Answer: Oxygen diffuses from lungs → alveoli → blood → hemoglobin carries it to cells. Nutrients absorbed from small intestine → blood → delivered to cells via circulatory system. Cells use oxygen and nutrients for respiration and energy production.

Question 5

HOTS

Define excretion. Name the main excretory organs in humans and one function of each.

Answer: Excretion removes metabolic wastes to maintain homeostasis.
Kidneys – filter blood and remove urea, salts, excess water.
Lungs – excrete CO₂.
Skin – remove water and salts via sweat.

Question 6

HOTS

Explain the role of stomata in plants. How do desert plants carry out photosynthesis with minimal water loss?

Answer: Stomata allow gas exchange (CO₂ in, O₂ out) and transpiration. Desert plants close stomata during day to conserve water and open at night to store CO₂ for photosynthesis (CAM pathway).
Example: Cactus, Aloe vera.

Question 7

HOTS

Explain “double circulation” in humans. Why is it necessary for efficient metabolism and high energy demand?

Answer: Double circulation: Blood passes twice through the heart – pulmonary (heart → lungs → heart) and systemic (heart → body → heart). Ensures oxygenated blood reaches body efficiently and deoxygenated blood returns to lungs for oxygenation. Supports high energy demand and efficient metabolism.
