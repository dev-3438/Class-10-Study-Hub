Advanced Questions - Light Reflection and Refraction

Question 1: Critical Thinking

Advanced

A student claims that if we use a convex lens of very large focal length, it will behave like a plane mirror. Do you agree? Justify your answer with proper reasoning and mathematical support.

Answer:

Analysis:

Yes, I agree with this claim. As the focal length of a convex lens increases significantly, its behavior approaches that of a plane mirror.

Mathematical Justification:
For a convex lens: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u

As f → ∞ (very large focal length):
1/∞ = 1/v - 1/u
0 = 1/v - 1/u
1/v = 1/u
Therefore: v = u

Magnification: m = v/u = u/u = +1
Physical Interpretation:
  • When f → ∞, the lens surface becomes almost flat
  • Image distance equals object distance (v = u)
  • Magnification = +1 (same size, erect image)
  • These are exactly the properties of a plane mirror!
  • The lens converges parallel rays at infinity, just like a plane mirror
Conclusion: A convex lens with infinite focal length behaves identically to a plane mirror in terms of image formation properties.

Question 2: Problem Solving

Advanced

A compound lens system consists of two lenses: a convex lens (f₁ = 20 cm) and a concave lens (f₂ = -30 cm) placed in contact. An object is placed 40 cm from this combination. Find the position and nature of the final image. Also, calculate the total power of this lens system.

Answer:

Step 1: Calculate Total Power
P₁ = 1/f₁ = 1/0.20 = +5 D
P₂ = 1/f₂ = 1/(-0.30) = -3.33 D
P_total = P₁ + P₂ = 5 - 3.33 = +1.67 D
Step 2: Find Equivalent Focal Length
f_eq = 1/P_total = 1/1.67 = 0.60 m = 60 cm
(Positive value indicates converging system)
Step 3: Find Image Position
Given: u = -40 cm, f = +60 cm
Using lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
1/60 = 1/v - 1/(-40)
1/60 = 1/v + 1/40
1/v = 1/60 - 1/40 = (2 - 3)/120 = -1/120
v = -120 cm
Step 4: Calculate Magnification
m = v/u = (-120)/(-40) = +3
Final Results:
• Total Power: +1.67 D
• Image Position: 120 cm on same side as object
• Nature: Virtual, erect, magnified 3 times
Key Insight: Even though we have both convex and concave lenses, the system acts as a converging lens because the convex lens has stronger power.

Question 3: Design Thinking

Advanced

Design an optical system using lenses that can project a sharp, magnified image of a small object (2 mm) onto a screen 2 meters away. The final image should be 20 cm high and inverted. Specify the type, position, and focal length of lenses required. Also explain the working principle.

Answer:

Requirements Analysis:
  • Object height: 2 mm = 0.2 cm
  • Image height: 20 cm
  • Required magnification: m = -20/0.2 = -100 (negative for inverted)
  • Image distance from lens: 2 m = 200 cm
Lens Selection and Calculation:
Using m = v/u: -100 = -200/u
Therefore: u = 2 cm (object distance)

Using lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
1/f = 1/200 - 1/2 = (1 - 100)/200 = -99/200
f = -200/99 = -2.02 cm

Problem: Negative focal length suggests concave lens, but we need real image!
Solution: Two-Lens System

Use a two-lens system:

  1. First Lens (Convex): f₁ = 2.1 cm, placed at 2.2 cm from object
  2. This creates intermediate image at 46.2 cm
  3. Second Lens (Convex): f₂ = 50 cm, placed to project final image at 200 cm
Working Principle:
• First lens creates magnified intermediate image
• Second lens further magnifies and projects onto screen
• Total magnification = m₁ × m₂ = (-21) × (-4.76) ≈ +100
• Final image is real, inverted, and 20 cm high
Design Specifications:
• Lens 1: Convex, f = 2.1 cm, placed 2.2 cm from object
• Lens 2: Convex, f = 50 cm, placed 50 cm from Lens 1
• Screen: 200 cm from Lens 2
• Total system length: ~252 cm

Question 4: Analytical Reasoning

Advanced

A beam of white light passes through a glass prism and gets dispersed into seven colors. If we replace the glass prism with a hollow prism filled with water, how will the dispersion pattern change? Explain the physics behind your answer. Also, calculate the angle of deviation for red and violet light if μ_red = 1.331 and μ_violet = 1.343 for water.

Answer:

Comparison of Dispersion:
Property Glass Prism Water Prism
Refractive Index Range 1.5 - 1.7 1.331 - 1.343
Dispersion Amount High Lower
Angle of Deviation Larger Smaller
Physics Explanation:
  • Dispersion depends on difference in refractive indices for different colors
  • Glass has higher refractive index and greater variation between colors
  • Water has lower refractive index and smaller variation
  • Therefore, water prism shows less dispersion than glass prism
  • The spectrum will be more compressed with water prism
Calculation of Angle of Deviation:

Assuming prism angle A = 60° and angle of incidence i = 45°:

For Red Light (μ = 1.331):
Using Snell's law at first surface: sin r₁ = sin i/μ = sin 45°/1.331
sin r₁ = 0.707/1.331 = 0.531
r₁ = 32.1°

At second surface: r₂ = A - r₁ = 60° - 32.1° = 27.9°
sin e = μ × sin r₂ = 1.331 × sin 27.9° = 1.331 × 0.467 = 0.622
e = 38.4°

Angle of deviation: δ = i + e - A = 45° + 38.4° - 60° = 23.4°
For Violet Light (μ = 1.343):
sin r₁ = sin 45°/1.343 = 0.707/1.343 = 0.526
r₁ = 31.8°

r₂ = 60° - 31.8° = 28.2°
sin e = 1.343 × sin 28.2° = 1.343 × 0.472 = 0.634
e = 39.3°

δ = 45° + 39.3° - 60° = 24.3°
Results:
• Red light deviation: 23.4°
• Violet light deviation: 24.3°
• Angular dispersion: 0.9°
• This is much smaller than glass prism dispersion (~2-3°)
Conclusion: Water prism produces less dispersion than glass prism due to smaller variation in refractive index with wavelength.
