Heredity & Evolution

Complete Class 10 Concepts

Chapter Video

1. What is Heredity?

Heredity: The transmission of traits (characteristics) from parents to offspring through genes.
Genetics: The science that studies heredity and variation.

2. Key Terms & Concepts

  • Gene: Segment of DNA controlling a trait; basic unit of heredity.
  • Alleles: Different forms of the same gene (e.g., flower color may have allele “white” or “purple”).
  • Genotype: Genetic makeup of an organism (which alleles it has).
  • Phenotype: Observable traits resulting from genotype + environment.
  • Variation: Differences between individuals of the same species. Arises due to alleles, mutations, recombination, environment.

3. Mendel’s Laws of Inheritance

  • Law of Dominance: One allele (dominant) masks the other (recessive).
  • Law of Segregation: Alleles segregate during gamete formation; each gamete carries only one allele.
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Alleles of different traits assort independently during gamete formation.

Use Punnett Squares to predict offspring traits.

4. Sex Determination in Humans

Humans have sex chromosomes: typically XX (female), XY (male).
Sex is determined during fertilization when gametes combine (egg + sperm).

5. Role of Heredity and Variation

Heredity ensures offspring resemble parents, while variation ensures differences — critical for adaptability and survival.
Variation allows species to respond to changing environments.

6. Evolution

Evolution: Gradual change in organisms over generations, leading to new species or varieties.

  • Natural Selection: Favorable traits survive and reproduce more (“survival of the fittest”).
  • Genetic Drift: Random changes in allele frequencies, especially in small populations.
  • Speciation: Isolated populations accumulate differences → may form new species.

Evidence of Evolution: Fossils, homologous & analogous structures, embryology, DNA & molecular data.

  • Divergent Evolution: One species → multiple descendant species.
  • Convergent Evolution: Unrelated species evolve similar traits independently.
  • Parallel Evolution: Species with similar ancestry evolve similar traits in similar environments.

Note: Evolution is not goal-driven; it’s about adaptation and survival, not “progress.”
