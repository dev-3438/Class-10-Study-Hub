Light - Reflection and Refraction

Class 10 NCERT | Complete Chapter Summary

Quick Revision Guide for Board Exams

Key Concepts

Light: Form of energy, speed in vacuum = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
Reflection: Bouncing back of light from polished surface
Refraction: Bending of light when changing medium
Refractive Index: μ = c/v (ratio of speeds)

Laws

Reflection Laws:

  • ∠i = ∠r (angle of incidence = angle of reflection)
  • Incident ray, normal, reflected ray in same plane

Refraction Laws (Snell's Law):

  • sin i/sin r = μ (constant)
  • All rays lie in same plane

Spherical Mirrors

Types:

  • Concave: Converges parallel rays
  • Convex: Diverges parallel rays

Key Terms:

  • Pole, Center of Curvature, Focus
  • Focal Length: f = R/2

Essential Formulas

Mirror Formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
Magnification: m = h'/h = -v/u
Lens Formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
Power: P = 1/f (in meters)
Refractive Index: μ = c/v

Lenses

Convex Lens:

  • Thicker at center, converges light
  • Forms real/virtual images depending on object position

Concave Lens:

  • Thinner at center, diverges light
  • Always forms virtual, erect, diminished images

Image Formation Rules

Mirror Sign Convention:

  • Distances in incident light direction = +ve
  • Distances opposite = -ve
  • Heights above axis = +ve, below = -ve

Image Interpretation:

  • m = +ve → Erect image
  • m = -ve → Inverted image
  • |m| > 1 → Magnified
  • |m| < 1 → Diminished

Applications

Concave Mirrors:
  • Shaving mirrors
  • Telescope mirrors
  • Solar concentrators
Convex Lenses:
  • Camera lenses
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Human eye lens

Quick Memory Tips

Convex Converges, Concave Diverges - applies to both mirrors and lenses
Real images - can be caught on screen, formed by actual intersection
Virtual images - cannot be caught on screen, formed by apparent intersection
Power units: Dioptre (D) = 1/meter

Exam Success Tips

  • Always follow sign convention carefully
  • Practice ray diagrams regularly
  • Remember: Real images are always inverted (except at C for mirrors)
  • Virtual images are always erect
  • For numerical problems, write given data first
  • Check if answer makes physical sense
