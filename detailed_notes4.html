Life Processes

Complete Class 10 Science Concepts

1. Nutrition

Definition: Process by which organisms take in food, convert it into simpler substances, and use it for energy, growth, repair, and maintenance.

Types of Nutrition:

  • Autotrophic: Organisms make their own food (plants, some algae/bacteria) using sunlight, water, CO₂, and minerals. Eg: Photosynthesis in chloroplasts.
  • Heterotrophic: Organisms depend on others for food. Examples: animals, fungi, many bacteria.

Autotrophic Nutrition → Photosynthesis:

  • Occurs in chloroplasts using chlorophyll.
  • Raw materials: CO₂ (from air), H₂O (from soil), sunlight.
  • Process: Light energy → chemical energy; water splits → H & O₂; CO₂ reduced → carbohydrates; Oxygen released.
  • Importance: Plants produce food for themselves and the ecosystem.

Heterotrophic Nutrition:

  • Types: Holozoic (ingestion → digestion → absorption → assimilation → egestion), Saprophytic (feeds on dead matter), Parasitic (feeds on living host).
  • Example: Amoeba uses pseudopodia → food vacuole → digestion → absorption → egestion.
  • Humans: Digestion occurs in digestive system (alimentary canal + glands: salivary, liver, pancreas, intestinal).

2. Respiration

Definition: Process of breaking down food to release energy for life processes.

Types of Respiration:

  • Aerobic: Requires oxygen; glucose → CO₂ + H₂O + energy (ATP). Most organisms use this.
  • Anaerobic: Occurs without oxygen; produces less energy. Seen in yeast, some bacteria, or brief human activity.

Human Respiration:

  • Respiratory system: Nose → pharynx → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → lungs → alveoli.
  • Breathing: Inhalation → diaphragm + intercostal muscles contract → air enters lungs. Exhalation → muscles relax → air expelled.
  • Gas exchange: O₂ diffuses into blood, CO₂ diffuses out; hemoglobin carries O₂ to tissues.

3. Transportation

Purpose: Transport nutrients, gases, and wastes to/from cells.

In Humans:

  • Circulatory system: Blood + heart + blood vessels.
  • Blood components: RBCs (O₂), WBCs (immune), platelets (clotting).
  • Heart: Pumps blood; double circulation (blood passes heart twice per cycle).
  • Blood vessels: Arteries (oxygenated blood), Veins (deoxygenated), Capillaries (exchange substances).

In Plants:

  • Xylem: Water & minerals from roots → leaves.
  • Phloem: Transports food from leaves → other parts.
  • Transpiration: Evaporation from leaves pulls water upward.
  • Translocation: Food transport in phloem.

4. Excretion

In Humans:

  • Excretory system: Kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra.
  • Filters metabolic wastes (urea, salts, water) from blood; maintains chemical balance.
  • Other waste removal: Lungs (CO₂), skin (sweat).

In Plants:

  • Wastes expelled through stomata/lenticels or stored in leaves/structures that fall off.

5. Importance of Life Processes

Life processes maintain life: energy production, nourishment, waste removal, transport, and homeostasis. Essential for survival, growth, reproduction, and adaptation. Understanding these processes is foundational for all biology topics.
