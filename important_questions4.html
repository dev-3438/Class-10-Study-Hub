Important Board Exam Questions

Life Processes (3-4 Marks Each)

Question 1

2 Marks

Define life processes. Name the basic life processes common to all living organisms.

Answer:

Life processes are the essential physiological functions that living organisms carry out to stay alive. Basic life processes common to all living beings include nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion.

Question 2

2 Marks

Give the balanced chemical equation for photosynthesis. Also name the raw materials required.

Answer:

Equation:
6CO2 + 6H2O → chlorophyll + sunlight → C6H12O6 + 6O2

Raw materials required: Carbon dioxide, water, sunlight, and chlorophyll (the pigment).

Question 3

2 Marks

Why is diffusion enough for transport and excretion in unicellular organisms but insufficient in multicellular organisms?

Answer:

In a unicellular organism, the single cell is in direct contact with the external environment, so diffusion suffices for exchange of gases, nutrients and wastes. But in multicellular organisms, many cells are not in direct contact with the environment — so diffusion alone can’t meet oxygen or nutrient demand or remove wastes. Hence they require specialised transport and excretory systems.

Question 4

2 Marks

Explain the term “double circulation” in human circulatory system. Why is it necessary?

Answer:

Double circulation means that blood passes twice through the heart in one complete cycle — first from heart → lungs → back to heart (pulmonary circulation), then from heart → body → back to heart (systemic circulation). It’s necessary because it ensures oxygenated blood gets efficiently delivered to all body parts and deoxygenated blood goes back to lungs for oxygenation. This supports high energy demands and efficient metabolism.

Question 5

2 Marks

What are the components of transport system in plants? Briefly describe their functions.

Answer:

The transport system in plants includes xylem and phloem. Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves, while phloem transports food (like sugars) from leaves to other parts of the plant.

Question 6

2 Marks

Define excretion. Name the main excretory organs in humans and one function of each.

Answer:

Excretion is the process of removing metabolic wastes from the body to maintain homeostasis. In humans:

  • Kidneys: filter blood to form urine, removing urea, salts & extra water.
  • Lungs: remove CO₂ from blood during exhalation.
  • Skin: through sweat removes some water and salts.

Question 7

2 Marks

Why do herbivores have longer small intestines than carnivores?

Answer:

Herbivores eat plant material rich in cellulose, which is harder to digest. A longer small intestine gives more time and surface area for digestion and absorption of such complex plant material. Carnivores eat easily digestible animal proteins/fats, so shorter intestine suffices.

Question 8

2 Marks

What happens when a human muscle works under low oxygen supply (during heavy exercise)? Why does cramp occur?

Answer:

Under low oxygen, muscles switch to anaerobic respiration — glucose breaks down to produce energy plus lactic acid. Lactic acid accumulation causes muscle fatigue and cramps.

Question 9

2 Marks

Describe how food is transported in human body after digestion and how oxygen reaches body cells.

Answer:

After digestion in the alimentary canal, nutrients are absorbed into blood through the small intestine, then transported via blood (in circulatory system) to various body cells. Oxygen uptake occurs in lungs via alveoli → diffuses into blood (via hemoglobin in RBCs) → transported by blood to all cells for respiration.

Question 10

2 Marks

What is the role of stomata in plants? How do desert plants manage photosynthesis despite water scarcity?

Answer:

Stomata are pores on leaf surfaces that allow gas exchange (CO₂ in, O₂ out) and transpiration (water vapor out). Desert plants often keep stomata closed during day to reduce water loss; some do photosynthesis at night (when stomata open) — storing CO₂ until daylight, then performing photosynthesis.

Important 5-Marks Questions

Question 1

5 Marks

Draw and explain the human excretory system. Describe the process of urine formation.

Answer:

The human excretory system consists of kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, and urethra.
Urine formation occurs in three steps:

  • Filtration: Blood enters nephrons via glomerulus; water and small solutes are filtered into Bowman’s capsule.
  • Reabsorption: Useful substances like glucose, amino acids, and water are reabsorbed back into blood from tubules.
  • Secretion: Waste products like urea, creatinine, and excess ions are secreted into tubules to form urine.

Question 2

5 Marks

Explain the process of transport of water, minerals, and food in plants with a labeled diagram.

Answer:

Transport in Plants:

  • Xylem: Transports water and minerals from roots to leaves through transpiration pull and root pressure.
  • Phloem: Transports food (mainly sucrose) from leaves to other parts of the plant by translocation.
  • Mechanism: Water moves by cohesion-tension, minerals by active transport, and food moves through pressure-flow hypothesis in phloem.

Question 3

5 Marks

Draw a labeled diagram of human circulatory system. Explain double circulation and its importance.

Answer:

Human Circulatory System: Consists of heart, blood, and blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries).
Double Circulation: Blood passes twice through the heart in one cycle:

  • Pulmonary Circulation: Heart → Lungs → Heart (for oxygenation)
  • Systemic Circulation: Heart → Body → Heart (oxygenated blood delivered to body)

Importance: Ensures efficient supply of oxygen and nutrients to all body parts and proper removal of wastes, supporting high metabolic demand.
