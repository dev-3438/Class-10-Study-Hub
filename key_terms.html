Key Terms & Definitions

Light - Reflection and Refraction (Class 10 NCERT)

Light

A form of energy that enables us to see objects around us.

Reflection

The bouncing back of light when it strikes a polished surface.

Spherical Mirror

A mirror whose reflecting surface is part of a hollow sphere.

Pole

The center point of the reflecting surface of a spherical mirror.

Center of Curvature

The center of the sphere of which the mirror is a part.

Radius of Curvature

The distance between the pole and center of curvature.

Focus

The point where parallel rays of light meet after reflection.

Focal Length

The distance between the pole and the principal focus.

Principal Axis

The straight line passing through the pole and center of curvature.

Magnification

The ratio of image height to object height.

Refraction

The bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.

Refractive Index

The ratio of speed of light in vacuum to speed of light in medium.

Lens

A transparent medium bounded by two surfaces, at least one curved.

Convex Lens

A lens that is thicker at the center than at the edges.

Concave Lens

A lens that is thinner at the center than at the edges.

Power of Lens

The reciprocal of focal length (in meters), measured in dioptres.

Optical Density

The ability of a medium to slow down light rays.

Principal Focus

The point where parallel rays converge or appear to diverge.
