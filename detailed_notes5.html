Environment & Ecosystem

Environment: All living (plants, animals, microbes) and non-living (air, water, soil, sunlight) components around us.

Ecosystem: Functional unit of living organisms interacting with abiotic environment.

Components:

  • Biotic: Producers, Consumers, Decomposers
  • Abiotic: Air, Water, Soil, Sunlight, Temperature, Climate

Energy Flow: Food Chain & Web

Food Chain: Linear sequence showing who-eats-whom (e.g., Grass → Deer → Lion)

Trophic Levels:

  • Producers → Primary Consumers → Secondary/Tertiary Consumers → Decomposers

Food Web: Interconnected food chains showing ecosystem relationships.

10% Law: Only 10% energy transfers to next level; rest lost as heat/metabolism.

Bio-magnification: Harmful chemicals accumulate and increase in higher trophic levels.

Waste & Its Management

Biodegradable Waste: Decomposes naturally (food scraps, paper, wood)

Non-Biodegradable Waste: Does not decompose naturally (plastics, metals)

Disposal Methods:

  • Recycling
  • Composting
  • Incineration
  • Landfills
  • Sewage Treatment

Human Activities & Environmental Issues

  • Pollution: Air, Water, Soil contamination from waste, chemicals, plastics
  • Ozone Layer Depletion: Caused by CFCs, leads to harmful UV radiation
  • Global Imbalance: Deforestation, overuse of pesticides, habitat destruction, soil & water degradation

Conservation & Protection

  • Separate biodegradable & non-biodegradable waste; recycle and compost
  • Minimize plastic & non-biodegradable use
  • Reduce pollution; treat sewage; avoid ozone-depleting substances
  • Understand energy flow and protect biodiversity

Key Terms

  • Environment: All living + non-living factors around organisms
  • Ecosystem: Functional unit of interacting organisms & abiotic factors
  • Producers: Organisms making own food (plants, phytoplankton)
  • Consumers: Herbivores, Carnivores, Omnivores
  • Decomposers: Break down dead matter (fungi, bacteria)
  • Food Chain/Web: Feeding relationships in ecosystem
  • Trophic Level: Steps in a food chain/web
  • Biodegradable Waste: Can be decomposed naturally
  • Non-Biodegradable Waste: Cannot decompose naturally
  • Bio-magnification: Increase of toxins in higher trophic levels
  • Ozone Layer: Protects Earth from UV rays
  • Ozone Depletion: Thinning of ozone due to chemicals like CFCs
