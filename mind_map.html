Light - Reflection and Refraction (Complete Chapter Overview)

LIGHT: Reflection & Refraction

REFLECTION

Laws of Reflection:
• ∠i = ∠r
• All rays in same plane
Types:
• Regular (smooth surface)
• Irregular (rough surface)
Spherical Mirrors:
• Concave (converging)
• Convex (diverging)
Key Terms:
• Pole, Center of Curvature
• Focus, Focal Length (f = R/2)
Mirror Formula:
1/f = 1/v + 1/u
Sign convention important!
Magnification:
m = h'/h = -v/u
+ve = erect, -ve = inverted

REFRACTION

Snell's Law:
sin i/sin r = μ (constant)
Incident ray, normal, refracted ray
Behavior:
• Rarer → Denser: bends towards normal
• Denser → Rarer: bends away from normal
Refractive Index:
μ = c/v
Higher μ = denser medium
Lenses:
• Convex (converging)
• Concave (diverging)
Lens Formula:
1/f = 1/v - 1/u
Different sign convention!
Power of Lens:
P = 1/f (in meters)
Unit: Dioptre (D)

APPLICATIONS


Human Eye
Convex lens system

Cameras
Convex lenses for focusing

Vehicle Mirrors
Convex mirrors for wider view

Telescopes
Combination of lenses

Microscopes
Multiple lens systems

Solar Concentrators
Concave mirrors

KEY FORMULAS

Reflection:

  • • 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
  • • m = h'/h = -v/u
  • • f = R/2

Refraction:

  • • sin i/sin r = μ
  • • μ = c/v
  • • 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
  • • P = 1/f

Memory Tip

Remember: "Convex Converges, Concave Diverges" - this applies to both mirrors and lenses!
